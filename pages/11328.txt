She said: “The Olympics has been central to my life for nearly 30 years. "As an athlete I felt first-hand the incredible influence and impact sport has on people’s lives.“During my time as chair of UK Sport, I have learned the power of collaboration as part of this impressive eco system that enables Olympic sport to flourish in the UK, and so I look forward to embarking on this next chapter.”Getty ImagesAs part of the GB Olympic rowing team, Dame Katherine trained at the Redgrave Pincent Rowing Lake in CavershamTaking on the position as chair of the British Olympics Organisation (BOA) makes Dame Katherine the first woman in the role since its establishment in 1905.A statement from Team GB described the athlete as a "highly respected figure in sport".Dame Katherine won gold at London 2012, with Anna Watkins in the double sculls, as well as silver medals in various events in Sydney, Athens, Beijing and Rio. After retiring from her 20-year rowing career, she was appointed as chair of UK Sport, where she has worked to create a new direction for Olympic and Paralympic sport. She will now finish her term with UK Sport, before moving into her role with BOA.Andy Anson, the CEO of the BOA, said: “Katherine is an esteemed figure in high-performance sport, recognised in the British sporting community as a steadfast leader as well as one of our greatest ever Olympians. "We are looking forward to working with her in the build-up to and during Milano-Cortina 2026 and Los Angeles 2028."You can follow BBC Berkshire on Facebook, X (Twitter), or Instagram.
